Physics Engine

A software component that attempts to simulate real world physics such as gravity, forces, and collisions. It involves many calculations to properly simulate motion and interactions of objects to provide players an immersive experience while playing. It can also be used to handle complex mechanics like destructible environments.

This is arguably the most essential component in video games as there is always some interactions between elements no matter how realistic the game is. But since it can be a complex process to implement, most games stick to the basics like gravity and collisions.
