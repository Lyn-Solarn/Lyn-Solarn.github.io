Graphics Rendering

Whether it be a 2D game or a 3D one, there are lots of rendering techniques that people use to create interesting visuals. Using elements such as shaders and texture mapping, programmers can change how objects appear on screen by defining the how light and textures look and behave. Depending on the way these elements are handled, video games can look more photorealistic or cartoony.

There are two types of rendering, Real-Time Rendering and Pre-Rendering. When developers are building interactive motion graphics, they will use Real-Time Rendering to generate images instantly. Although it does require having dedicated graphics hardware to improve the performance as weaker graphic cards have a slower rendering process. Pre-Rendering though is primarily used in the motion picture industry to produce highly realistic images and effects.
