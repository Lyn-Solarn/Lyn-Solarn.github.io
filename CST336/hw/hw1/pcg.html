Procedural Content Generation (PCG)

This is a method of creating data using algorithms and rules rather than manually creating every element in a video game. It combines the randomness of particular algorithms - such as Perlin Noise, L-Systems, etc. - and logic given by humans to produce a diverse range of outputs. PCG provides the benefit of less repetitive gameplay, higher replay value, and reduced file sizes.

This generation of content can then be used for a variety of components in video games. Commonly is it used for terrain, animations, dialogue, object instantiation, and loot systems. Really anything that may benefit from a bit of randomness to add interest can be made with PCG techniques.
